# Round Recall — Logo, Step 2: refine the two finalists (still B&W)

Great thumbnail sheet. Two marks advance. Keep everything **black-on-white,
flat, no color, no polish** for this step. We are isolating ONE variable:
construction, weight, and proportion. Color comes later.

## The two finalists

- **2b "Arc-R"** — an R whose leg is a golf-shot flight arc. The geometric
  monogram lane (Spotify/Manus feel).
- **4c "Peek"** — a golf-ball mascot peeking over the green with two eyes. The
  mascot lane (Snap/Reddit feel).

Treat these as two separate brand personalities. Do not merge them.

## What to produce

For **each** finalist, a refinement row of **5 variants** exploring one axis at a
time, all monochrome:

**Arc-R:**
1. Vary the stroke weight: thin / medium / heavy / very heavy.
2. Vary how much the leg reads as an arc vs. a normal R leg — push the "this is a
   flight path" read without breaking letter legibility.
3. One version where the ball sits at the end of the arc as a small filled dot.
4. Test geometric (drawn on a strict grid, circular bowl) vs. slightly humanist.
5. Show the winner of each row at 16 / 28 / 64px and knocked-out white on a dark
   square.

**Peek:**
1. Vary eye size and spacing (too-wide reads goofy, too-close reads nervous —
   find friendly).
2. Vary how much ball shows above the line (sliver / half / two-thirds).
3. One version with a single dimple or subtle dimple hint, one with none.
4. Vary the "green" baseline: straight line vs. gentle curve (horizon of a
   putting green).
5. Show the winner at 16 / 28 / 64px and white-on-dark.

## Rules

- Build on a visible construction grid where it helps; consistent geometry beats
  freehand.
- Every variant must still pass: (a) one flat color, (b) legible at 16px,
  (c) holes are true negative space.
- No gradients, shadows, gloss, or color. Container is just a plain square for
  the icon test — don't style it.

## Deliverable

Two refinement rows (one per finalist), each ending in a "best of row" call-out
shown at the four sizes. Tell me which you'd pick and why — then we lock one and
move to color.
