# Round Recall — Positioning

> The name is locked: **Round Recall**. `roundrecall.com` is the canonical
> domain. This file is the source of truth for what the product is and how to
> talk about it. When the pitch and the product drift apart, fix one of them.

## The one-liner

Round Recall is a golf analytics app you fill out from memory: tap through
every shot of a round in under five minutes and see, in strokes gained, where
the round actually went.

## Positioning statement

For a serious golfer who plays the same course on repeat, Round Recall turns
five minutes of after-round memory into a per-shot strokes-gained breakdown
against a scratch baseline. Unlike GPS and sensor apps that tax you during the
round to log data you never revisit, Round Recall asks nothing on the course
and pays you back with a diagnosis you can act on: which link in the chain —
tee, approach, short game, putting — actually cost you, hole by hole.

## The wedge

Memory is the input. No GPS, no sensors, no live entry. That single constraint
is the product:

- It makes logging painless, so rounds actually get logged.
- It keeps the data clean and consistent, because one player enters it the
  same way every time.
- Played on one course forever, that history can become the optimal-reference
  baseline for those exact greens — a dataset no commercial app can match,
  because none has clean retrospective shot data for one player on one course.

The constraint is the moat. "Recall" names it.

## Why the name holds

"Recall" does two jobs at once. It names the *mechanic* — you reconstruct the
round from memory — and the *value* — the app is retrospective, it tells you
what happened, not what to do mid-swing. "Round" scales the concept to the
whole-round attribution that is the killer screen, and pairs into a clean,
alliterative phrase that is ownable as an exact-match .com.

It is deliberately not built to pass the "instantly obvious it's golf" test
(the 18Birdies bar). That test matters for apps fighting for cold app-store
downloads. Round Recall is met in context — inside the welcome overlay, as a
portfolio piece — so the name's job is to *reward* understanding, not to
*announce* the category. Different job, different name.

## Domino: the metaphor, not the name

A golf round is a line of dominoes — each shot sets up the next, and a stroke
lost early shows up as a worse position later. That image is the best way to
*explain* the engine: the putting "leak" was largely an approach-proximity
problem one link upstream, and strokes gained traces the stroke back to the
link that caused it. Use "dominoes" in copy and section headings. Do not use
it as the product name: it's owned by a pizza chain and a board game, it's
unwinnable in search, and it explains a concept the user only values *after*
they understand SG. It sells the room; "Recall" sells the door.

## What it is not

- Not a live-tracking or GPS app. Nothing is logged on the course.
- Not a coaching app that tells you what to do mid-round. It is a diagnosis,
  after the fact.
- Not a multi-course leaderboard product. v1 is one player, one course; the
  repeated-course constraint is a feature, not a limitation to grow out of.
